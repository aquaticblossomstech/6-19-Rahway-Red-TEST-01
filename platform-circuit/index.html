Circuit Interface // Cranford · Westfield · Summit

The Platform Circuit

Three Union County commuter towns that look nearly identical on a "best towns to raise a family" list and read completely differently once you trace what the train station actually does to each downtown. Cranford's commercial core is wedged between the Rahway River and the Raritan Valley Line platform. Westfield's downtown has grown into a regional shopping destination that pulls well past its own town line. Summit's Midtown Direct service gives it a one-seat ride to Manhattan that does an outsized amount of work in defining who lives there and what it costs. This circuit reads what "desirable" is actually built out of, town by town.

Why This Circuit Reads Differently

Cranford, Westfield, and Summit aren't overlooked the way some circuits' towns are — real estate blogs, school-ranking lists, and "best downtowns in New Jersey" roundups cover all three constantly. What's missing isn't attention. It's structure. All three are absorbing the same statewide pressure — train-adjacent redevelopment tied to New Jersey's decades-old affordable housing framework, the one that traces back to a court case named after a town well south of here — differently enough that the "best towns" framing never has to account for it.
